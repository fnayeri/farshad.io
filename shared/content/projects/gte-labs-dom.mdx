Foundational distributed-systems work in the Modula-3 lineage at GTE Laboratories. The Distributed Object Manager (DOM) provided the substrate for distributed-object research, published at the European Conference on Object-Oriented Programming (ECOOP) in 1994.

## What it studied

How to make distributed objects feel like local objects, without losing the explicitness about failure modes that distributed systems demand. Modula-3's threading and runtime were the native vocabulary.

## What it produced

The ECOOP 1994 publication and a working DOM implementation that informed the engineering culture I carried into Critical Mass Inc. the following year.

## Why it belongs

The cleanest evidence of the research-engineering register that runs through everything since: long horizons, careful written records, language and runtime as first-class design objects.
