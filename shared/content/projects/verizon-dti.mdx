Verizon's existing network monitoring gave a hardware-centric view. The system we built gave a customer-centric view: Quality of Service, end-to-end, across diverse network technologies, in real time.

## The architecture

A metadata-driven aggregation layer that ingested metrics from heterogeneous network components and presented a single QoS surface. Visualization components — tables, charts, time-series — were composable: NOC staff could spin up a view for a new investigation without engineering rework.

## What it shipped

The system ran across Verizon's full footprint. Network Operating Centers used it to reduce troubleshooting times and to spot patterns the per-component dashboards could not surface.

## Why it belongs in this list

Two reasons. First, it was a serious systems engagement at scale — ingestion, computation, visualization, all under tight latency budgets. Second, the visualization layer was an early example of treating real-time data as something to be *directed*, not merely displayed. The Pixxa lineage runs through this work.
