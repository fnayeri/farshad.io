Airshow was a stage piece. Two operators, one screen, ninety minutes of directed data about the most consequential consumer-technology company in history. The format was as much theater as analysis.

## The cities

Boston (the home opener), Tokyo, Vancouver, London, San Francisco, Helsinki, Seattle, Melbourne. Eight stops between 2013 and 2016. Audiences ranged from senior engineers at conferences (YOW! Connected, Melbourne, 2016) to investors and operators booking private engagements.

## The format

Each show was a three-act narrative. The opening act established the frame: who Apple was, where the numbers came from, what we were about to look at. The middle act ran the analysis live — Horace narrated, I directed the data. The third act was the implication: what the numbers said about where the company was heading.

The visualizations were not decks. They were *scenes*, directed in real time on Pixxa's Perspective platform.

## What it established

Airshow proved the craft. After the tour, "data cinematography" stopped being a phrase and became a thing operators could ask for by name.

The lineage continues: HarvardXR 2025, the Global Health Exhibition 2025, the Saudi Ministry of Health 2026.
