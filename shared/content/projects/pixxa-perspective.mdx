Pixxa was started to do one thing: bring the craft of cinematography to data presentation. Static charts kill the narrative. The hand on the focus ring — what enters frame, what leaves frame, when the cut comes — is the difference between a deck and a story.

## The product

**Perspective** is the iPad app at the center of the platform. The presenter directs the data: zoom into a country, dim the rest of the world, hold for a beat, cut to a multi-decade trend that recontextualizes what the audience just saw. The app is a directing surface; the data is the cast.

## Where it has been used

In live keynotes and high-stakes internal presentations at:

- Apple
- Samsung
- IBM
- Fidelity
- BNY Mellon
- Microsoft

Microsoft evaluated the underlying technology for acquisition.

## The platform behind it

Behind Perspective is a multi-tenant, API-driven cloud that ingests heterogeneous data, normalizes it across schemas, and produces the directable scene graphs the iPad app renders. The architecture has powered enterprise applications across financial services, public health, and consumer technology.

The platform is currently being evolved toward AI-driven and spatial-computing (visionOS) workflows.

## What it taught

Treating motion as a narrative tool — not a decoration — changes what data can do in a room. Chart.js makes a chart. A cinematographer makes a *scene*. Pixxa is the platform for the second thing.
