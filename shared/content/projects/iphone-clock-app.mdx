The early App Store rewarded operators who could go from idea to shipped binary in weeks. iGEN ran a small studio shipping consumer apps in that window, with the understanding that the strategy was emergent and the learnings compounded.

## The work

Over 100 apps released across the early App Store. Categories ranged from utilities (clocks, calculators, productivity) to consumer entertainment to small reference and education titles. The economics were B2C: Apple's distribution carried the apps to global audiences without B2B friction.

## What stuck

Multiple apps reached "Top Apps" placements in their categories. Cumulative downloads ran into the millions.

## What it taught

The App Store's first decade was an experiment in whether individual operators with good taste could compete with established software companies on consumer surface area. They could. The lesson generalizes: when distribution is solved, taste and shipping cadence are the moat.
