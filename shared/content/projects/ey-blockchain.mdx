EY Global Innovation needed a subscription-platform business to augment its consulting practice. The technical bet was a federated microservice blockchain platform on Azure that could be consumed by both existing and new applications across EY's many subsidiaries.

## The political problem

EY is a federation. Subsidiaries with conflicting interests must all be able to use the same platform without one of them owning the others. The architecture had to encode that constraint.

## The architecture

A federated microservice platform on Azure, with the front-end applications decoupled from the platform via well-bounded APIs. Authentication and metering became platform-level services, not per-application concerns.

## What shipped

A robust blockchain API platform, an accompanying website, and a full launch and migration plan. The platform served as the foundation for EY's subscription-services portfolio under the Global Innovation banner.

## What it taught

When the customer is a federation, the architecture is the negotiation. Get the boundaries right and the politics solve themselves.
