Led conception and development of Pixxa, a data visualization platform and SaaS cloud for interactive exploration of complex datasets. Built core technology for motion-based data visualization (Pixxa Perspective), delivering an end-to-end system spanning ingestion, transformation, and visualization across heterogeneous data models. Defined a scalable, multi-tenant, API-driven architecture used in enterprise applications at Apple, Samsung, IBM, Fidelity, BNY Mellon, and Microsoft. Currently evolving the platform toward AI-driven and spatial computing (VisionOS) workflows; technology was evaluated by Microsoft for acquisition.

## Problem
Complex datasets are difficult to explore and communicate using static tools. Existing solutions lack interactivity, scalability, and flexibility across heterogeneous data sources.

## Stakes
Poor data comprehension limits decision-making in enterprise environments. Without scalable, reusable platforms, organizations face high costs and fragmented tooling.

## Discovery
Combining real-time rendering, cloud-based data services, and motion-driven interaction enables more intuitive exploration of complex data. Multi-tenant architecture and API-first design allow reuse across industries and use cases.

## Outcome
Delivered a production platform powering interactive data applications across major enterprises. Established reusable architecture for scalable visualization systems.
